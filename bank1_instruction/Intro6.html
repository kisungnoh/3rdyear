Summary
  1. You will start a game with a randomly paired participant, who is likely to change from game to game.
  2. The bank state is randomly determined with the following probabilities:
    • Low with 15% probability
    • Medium with 70% probability
    • High with 15% probability
  3. Based on the bank state, the payoff table is displayed. The initials in the table represent the combination of decisions across two periods.
    • W: Withdraw (Period 1)
    • SW: Stay (Period 1) → Withdraw (Period 2)
    • SS: Stay (Period 1) → Stay (Period 2)
  4. Given the payoff table, you and the paired participant make decisions up to two periods:
    • In Period 1, both participants simultaneously choose either to withdraw or to stay.
    • If a participant chooses to stay in Period 1, they will move to Period 2, where they can choose again whether to withdraw or stay.
  5. At the end of each game, you will be told how many points you received, along with the other participant's decision.
  6. At the end of the experiment, one game is randomly selected for payment (1 point = {{C.REAL_WORLD_CURRENCY_PER_POINT}} dollar).
