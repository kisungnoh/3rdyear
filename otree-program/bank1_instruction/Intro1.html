General Information

In today's experiment, you will act as a depositor in a bank and make decisions for a number of matches.

You will be paired with another participant at the beginning of a match, and you and the other pair member are the only depositors in your bank. Pairs are randomly determined and are likely to change each match. Part 1 consists of 3 matches.


How a Match Proceeds
Stage 1 Stage 2 (label for each path)
You:
• action A : A
• action B
• action A : BA
• action B : BB

You will start a match with 10 points in your deposit account and make decisions over two stages.

In Stage 1, both you and the other depositor simultaneously choose between action A and action B.

  • If you choose action A, your decisions end: you do not proceed to Stage 2. (Path label: A)
  • If you choose action B, you move to Stage 2 and choose again between action A and action B.
    • Choosing action A in Stage 2 is labeled BA.
    • Choosing action B in Stage 2 is labeled BB.

Suppose you reached Stage 2.

  • If the other depositor also chose action B in Stage 1, both of you will be making the Stage 2 decision simultaneously again.
  • If the other depositor chose action A in Stage 1, you will be the only one in Stage 2.
However, you will NOT see what the other depositor chose in Stage 1. Thus, you will not know which case applies.

On the next page, you will see the payoff table showing how many points you earn based on both of your decisions.
