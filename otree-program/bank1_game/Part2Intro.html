Earnings

This part lasts for {{ part2_rounds }} matches. At the end of today's experiment, the computer will randomly select two matches for payment.

  • In one match, you will earn points from the decision you make, as in Part 1.
  • In a second match, you will earn either 0 or 4 points, based on the belief you reported about the other's decision.

Your payoffs from the selected matches will then be displayed and converted to dollars at the rate of 1 point = {{C.REAL_WORLD_CURRENCY_PER_POINT}} dollar.

The payment rule for belief reporting is designed in a way that honestly reporting your true assessment always maximizes your chance of winning 4 points.

Here's why: your earnings from belief reporting will depend on the actual action the other depositor chose. This means that reporting a higher likelihood for an action increases your chance of earning points if that action actually occurs, but decreases your chance if it does not.

Since you do not know which match will be selected for payment, it is in your best interest to report your beliefs truthfully in every match.

If you'd like to know more about how payments are calculated, click here.

Payment Rule for Belief Reporting

To determine your payment, the computer will randomly draw two numbers. Each number is between 0 and 100, and all numbers in this range are equally likely to be drawn. The two draws are independent, meaning that the outcome of one draw does not influence the other.

  • If the other depositor chose action A in Stage 1: you will be rewarded if the number you submitted as the likelihood that the other chose action A is larger than either one of the two draws.
  • If the other depositor chose action B in Stage 1: you will be rewarded if the number you submitted as the likelihood that the other chose action B is larger than either one of the two draws.
