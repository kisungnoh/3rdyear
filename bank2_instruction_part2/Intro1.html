Part 2 describes today's experiment. In this part, you will act as a depositor in a bank and make decisions for 10 matches. While most of the structure remains the same as Part 1 (previous experiment), there are some differences. Below section describes the similarities first. The next page describes the differences (new features).


Similarities

You will be paired with another participant at the beginning of a match. You and the other pair member are the only depositors in your bank. Pairs are randomly assigned in each match, so you will likely be paired with a different participant each time.

You will start a match with 10 points in your deposit account and make decisions (action A or B) over two stages. You will receive different payoffs depending on the state of your bank, your action, and the other's action.
