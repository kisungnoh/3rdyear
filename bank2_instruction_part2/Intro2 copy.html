Today's experiment is similar to Part 1, but with a key difference.

Note: Any quantitative information below (tables) will be shown to you when you make decisions. You do not need to memorize the numbers.


Bank State Is Unknown

In the previous experiment (Part 1), participants each played 40 games. In each game, two participants made decisions at a bank. That bank had a state (Low, Medium, or High), and participants knew the state when making decisions.

In today's experiment, you are also a depositor in a bank. Unlike the previous experiment, however, you will not be told the state of your bank. You need to make decisions without knowing whether your bank is in a Low, Medium, or High state.

Instead, your bank's state is determined as follows:

  • At the start of each game, one game from the previous experiment is randomly picked. The bank from that past game will be called the Pre-Bank to distinguish it from your bank in today's experiment.
  • Your bank's state is then randomly determined based on the Pre-Bank's state, as shown in the table below.

  • The probability of each Pre-Bank state may give you a clue about what state your bank is likely to be in.
