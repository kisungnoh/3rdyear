Differences (New features)

In the previous experiment, participants made decisions as bank depositors in 40 matches. In each of today's matches, one of those 40 matches will be randomly selected, and you will see what happened to the bank. Since this bank comes from the previous experiment, we call it the Pre-Bank. The bank you are assigned to today is referred to as your bank. Note that a new Pre-Bank will be randomly drawn in each match today.

Below describes three new features in today's experiment.


How the State Is Determined

IMPORTANT: Unlike the previous experiment, you will NOT know the state of your bank when you make your decisions. You will not know the state of the Pre-Bank, either. Thus, you should consider that your bank may be in any one of the three states when making decisions.

You do not observe the bank states in today's experiment, but you will be given other piece of information that may help you make your decisions.

In Stage 1, you will see the A-count: the number of depositors in the Pre-Bank who chose action A in either Stage 1 (A) or Stage 2 (BA). Each bank had two depositors, so the A-count can be 0, 1, or 2.

This information may help you get a better idea of the Pre-Bank's state, which may also help you infer the likely state of your bank. Recall that the state of Pre-Bank and that of your bank are related, as described in 1) How the State is Determined.

Note: A new Pre-Bank is randomly selected each match today, and the A-count you see each time is likely to be from different participants from the previous experiment.
