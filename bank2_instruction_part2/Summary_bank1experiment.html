Summary of the Previous Experiment
  1. Each game started with a randomly paired participant, who was likely to change from game to game.
  2. The bank state was randomly determined with the following probabilities:
    • Low with 15% probability
    • Medium with 70% probability
    • High with 15% probability
  3. Once the game started, the payoff table for the current bank state was displayed. Initials represented possible decisions across two periods.
    • W: Withdraw (Period 1)
    • SW: Stay (Period 1) → Withdraw (Period 2)
    • SS: Stay (Period 1) → Stay (Period 2)
  4. Given the payoff table, participants made decisions up to two periods:
    • In Period 1, both participants simultaneously chose either to withdraw or to stay.
    • If a participant chose to stay in Period 1, they moved to Period 2, where they could choose again to withdraw or stay.
  5. At the end of each game, participants were told how many points they received, along with the other participant's decision.
  6. Participants played 40 games in total. At the end of the experiment, they were paid in cash based on their outcomes.
