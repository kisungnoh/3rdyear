Earnings

This part lasts for {{ part3_rounds }} matches. At the end of today's experiment, the computer will randomly select three matches for payment.

  • In one match, you will earn points from the decision you make, as in Part 2.
  • In a second match, you will earn either 0 or 4 points, based on your belief about the other's Stage 1 action (Task 1 above).
  • In a third match, you will earn either 0 or 4 points, based on your belief about the state of your bank (Task 2 above).

Your Task 3 report is not used for payment. It is collected for research purposes only.

Your payoffs from the selected matches will be displayed and converted to dollars at the rate of 1 point = {{C.REAL_WORLD_CURRENCY_PER_POINT}} dollar.

The payment rule for belief reporting is designed in a way that honestly reporting your true assessment always maximizes your chance of winning 4 points.

Here's why: your earnings from belief reporting will depend on the actual outcome (the other depositor's action for Task 1, and the actual bank state for Task 2). This means that reporting a higher likelihood for an outcome increases your chance of earning points if that outcome actually occurs, but decreases your chance if it does not.

Since you do not know which match will be selected for payment, it is in your best interest to report your beliefs truthfully in every match.

If you'd like to know more about how payments are calculated, click here.

Payment Rule for Task 1 (the Other's Stage 1 Action)

To determine your payment, the computer will randomly draw two numbers. Each number is between 0 and 100, and all numbers in this range are equally likely to be drawn. The two draws are independent, meaning that the outcome of one draw does not influence the other.

  • If the other depositor chose action A in Stage 1: you will be rewarded if the likelihood you reported for action A is larger than either one of the two draws.
  • If the other depositor chose action B in Stage 1: you will be rewarded if the likelihood you reported for action B is larger than either one of the two draws.

If this rule sounds confusing,

Simply remember: submitting your belief truthfully will maximize your chance of winning 4 points.
Payment Rule for Task 2 (the Bank State)

To determine your payment, the computer will first randomly pick one of the three states: Low, Medium, or High. Then, the computer will randomly draw two numbers. Each number is between 0 and 100, and all numbers in this range are equally likely to be drawn. The two draws are independent, meaning that the outcome of one draw does not influence the other.

Suppose the computer picks the Low state, and you submitted 29% as the likelihood of Low, which means 71% for NOT Low (i.e., Medium or High).

  • If the actual state was Low in the selected match: you will get rewarded if the likelihood you reported for Low state (29%) is larger than either one of the two draws.
  • If the actual state was Medium or High in that match: you will get rewarded if the likelihood you reported for your bank NOT in Low state (71%) is larger than either one of the two draws.

The same logic applies when the computer picksthe Medium or High state.

If this rule sounds confusing,

Simply remember: submitting your belief truthfully will maximize your chance of winning 4 points.
