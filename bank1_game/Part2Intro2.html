Earnings
This part lasts for {{ part2_rounds }} games. At the end of today's experiment, the computer will randomly select two games for payment.

  • In one game, you will earn points from the decision you make, as in Part 1.
  • In a second game, you will earn either 0 or 4 points, based on the belief you reported about the other's Period 1 choice.

Your payoffs from the selected games will then be displayed and converted to dollars at the rate of 1 point = {{C.REAL_WORLD_CURRENCY_PER_POINT}} dollar.

The payment rule for belief reporting is designed in a way that honestly reporting your true assessment always maximizes your chance of winning 4 points.

Here's why: your earnings from belief reporting will depend on the actual action the other participant chose (withdraw or stay). This means that reporting a higher likelihood for an action increases your chance of earning points if that action actually occurs, but decreases your chance if it does not.

Since you do not know which game will be selected for payment, it is in your best interest to report your beliefs truthfully in every game.

Payment Rule for Belief Reporting

To determine your payment, the computer will randomly draw two numbers. Each number is between 0 and 100, and all numbers in this range are equally likely to be drawn. The two draws are independent, meaning that the outcome of one draw does not influence the other.

  • If the other participant chose to withdraw in Period 1: you will be rewarded if your reported percentage of withdrawal is larger than either one of the two draws.
  • If the other participant chose to stay in Period 1: you will be rewarded if your reported percentage of staying is larger than either one of the two draws.
