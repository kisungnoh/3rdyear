Summary
  1. In each game, a participant was randomly paired with another person. The pair was likely different each game.
  2. The bank state was randomly determined and known to both depositors at the start of each game:
    • Low with 15% probability
    • Medium with 70% probability
    • High with 15% probability
  3. Each depositor chose between action A and action B in Stage 1. Choosing action B led to Stage 2, where they chose again. This produced three possible action combinations: A, BA, or BB.
  4. When a depositor reached Stage 2, they did not know the other depositor's action in Stage 1. That is, they did not know whether the other depositor had also reached Stage 2.
  5. At the end of each game, both depositors were told their points and the other's action.

These instructions describe the previous experiment, which is related to today's experiment.
