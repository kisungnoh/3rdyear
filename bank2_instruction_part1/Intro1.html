Recently, [X] people participated in an experiment and played games in a pair. In each game, they acted as depositors in a bank. In today's experiment, you will also act as a depositor and play similar games. To do well on today's game, it is important to understand the previous experiment.

In Part 1, you will first learn about the game other people played. Afterward, you will receive instructions for today's game.

Previous Experiment

Pairing

Participants played 40 games in total. In each game, a participant was randomly paired with another person. Thus, the pair member was likely to change each game. The two participants in each pair were the only depositors in their bank.

Bank State

The bank could be in one of three states: Low, Medium, or High. The state was randomly drawn with the following probabilities:

  • Low with 15% probability
  • Medium with 70% probability
  • High with 15% probability
Both depositors were informed of the bank state when the game started.

Decisions

Each depositor started a game with 10 points in their deposit account. Then, they chose between action A and action B over two stages, which earned them different points.

  • Stage 1: Both depositors simultaneously chose between action A and action B.
    • Depositor who chose action A → their decision-making ended.
    • Depositor who chose action B → they moved to Stage 2 and chose again between action A and action B.

Below figure shows the decision flow.

Each combination of actions was labeled: A if they chose action A in Stage 1; BA if they chose action B in Stage 1 and then action A in Stage 2; or BB if they chose action B in both stages.

IMPORTANT: In Stage 2, a depositor could NOT observe the other's Stage 1 choice. In other words, they did not know whether the person they were paired with had also proceeded to Stage 2 or not.
