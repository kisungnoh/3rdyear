In today's experiment, you will be randomly paired with another participant and make decisions as a depositor in a bank. You will receive information to help your decision-making, which relates to a similar experiment that other students recently participated in. In Part 1, you will learn about the game they played in the previous experiment. You will then be given instructions for today's experiment in Part 2.


General Information

In the previous experiment, each participant acted as a depositor in a bank and made decisions for 40 matches.

A participant was paired with another at the beginning of a match, and the two participants were the only depositors in their bank. The pair was determined randomly and was likely to change each match.


How a Match Proceeded
Stage 1 Stage 2 (label for each path)
Depositor:
• action A : A
• action B
• action A : BA
• action B : BB

Participants started a match with 10 points in their deposit account, and they could make decisions over two stages.

In Stage 1, both depositors simultaneously chose between action A and action B.

  • If a depositor chose action A, their decisions ended: they did not proceed to Stage 2 (labeled A).
  • If a depositor chose action B, they moved to Stage 2 and chose again between action A and action B.
    • Choosing action A in Stage 2 was labeled BA.
    • Choosing action B in Stage 2 was labeled BB.

Observations:

  • If both depositors chose action A in Stage 1, neither moved to Stage 2.
  • If both depositors chose action B in Stage 1, both moved to Stage 2 and made the second decision simultaneously again.
  • If only one depositor chose action B in Stage 1, only that depositor moved to Stage 2.
However, they could NOT see what the other depositor chose in Stage 1. Thus, they did not know which case applied.

On the next page, you will see the payoff table showing how many points participants earned based on their decisions.
