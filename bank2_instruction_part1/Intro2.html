As the state went from Low to High, depositors could earn more points.
▶ hover a line to highlight the state

  • In the Low state, a depositor could receive between 0 and 10 points.
  • In the Medium state, a depositor could receive between 0 and 18 points.
  • In the High state, a depositor could receive between 10 and 18 points.

In each game, the bank state was randomly chosen: Low (15%), Medium (70%), High (15%).

Each depositor was told the exact bank state before making decisions. They only saw the payoff table for their state.

Here is how each action affected a depositor's payoff.
▶ hover a line to highlight the table row

  • Action A in Stage 1 (A): always received {{C.ENDOWMENT}} points, regardless of the bank state or the other's action.
  • Action B in Stage 1, then Action A in Stage 2 (BA): received either {{delayed_a}} or {{C.ENDOWMENT}} points, depending on the bank state and the other's action.
    • In the Low state: always received {{delayed_a}}.
    • In the High state: always received {{C.ENDOWMENT}}.
    • In the Medium state: received {{C.ENDOWMENT}} if the other depositor chose BB; otherwise received {{delayed_a}}.
  • Action B in Stage 1, then Action B in Stage 2 (BB): received either {{C.FAIL_PAYOFF}} or {{C.SUCCESS_PAYOFF}} points, depending on the bank state and the other's action.
    • In the Low state: always received {{C.FAIL_PAYOFF}}.
    • In the High state: always received {{C.SUCCESS_PAYOFF}}.
    • In the Medium state: received {{C.SUCCESS_PAYOFF}} if the other depositor also chose BB; otherwise received {{C.FAIL_PAYOFF}}.
After Making Decisions

At the end of each game, both depositors were told their points and the other's action. They were then randomly paired with another person for the next game. Participants received payment at the end of the experiment.
